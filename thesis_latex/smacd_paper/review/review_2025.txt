----------- Overall evaluation -----------
SCORE: 1 (weak accept)
----- TEXT:
The paper proposes a fuzzy logic controller-based MPPT algorithm integrated with an advanced synchronous buck converter model, also addressing addresses the key nonlinearities and uncertainties typical in real-world PV systems.
The modeling and simulation are verified in MATLAB/Simulink. However, the paper doesn't provide experimental validation or hardware implementation.. Efficiency and power losses analysis are missing.
Some references in the text to figures or tables (e.g., Table ?? on page 3) appear incomplete.
Crucial considerations:
- have the AUthors tried to implement quantitative comparison with other MPPT methods?
- how do the Authros plan to integrate the prospective IC implementation in a real commercial PV battery system?

----- TEXT:
The paper discusses a topic of great interest, regarding the Maximum Power Point Tracking in PV systems. Unfortunately, there are several points that make the paper quite weak for the presentation as is in the conference. In the following, the comments.

Minor comments

1) what is the battery voltage? 12V?

2) page 2, left column, section II, sub-section A.2): how is the efficiency of the PV system defined?

3) page 3, left column, section II, sub-section B.1): “Table ??” should be “Table I”.

Major comments

1) which are the characteristics of the buck converter? which are power MOSFETs, gate driver settings, power inductor, power capacitors, and switching frequency? how have these components been selected?…the efficiency of the buck converter strongly depends on power components and operating conditions. The components must be selected to fit the voltage and current characterizing the operating cycle of the PV system in order to achieve the best efficieny performance.

Please add information and discussion

2) the statement on page 2, left column, section II, sub-section A.1) “… Beyond this point, further increases in the duty cycle lead to a decrease in output current due to non-ideal effects such as switching losses and inductor saturation, as given in Figure 3.” looks not convincing. If the plot of Fig.3 provides the output current of the buck converter fed by the PV panel 6MN6A280 and loaded by a 12V battery, then the increase of the buck duty-cycle involves a decrease of the PV panel voltage. Therefore, the output current peak observed in Fig.3 around D=0.55 seems to correspond to the maximum power point of the PV panel. The output current decrease observed while the duty increases after that peak looks more realistically and mainly due to the decrease of the PV panel output power, rather than to "non-ideal effects such as switching losses and inductor saturation".

Please verify and clarify

3) page 2, left column, section II, sub-section A.2): the statement “Beyond this point, efficiency decreases due to increased losses in the converter.” looks not convincing for the same reason given above in comment 2).

Please verify and clarify

4) page 2, right column, section II, subsection A.3): the following statements “At low duty cycles, the converter efficiency is low due to high conduction losses. As the duty cycle increases, the efficiency improves, reaching a maximum at the optimal duty cycle, as given in Figure 5. Beyond this point, switching losses and inductor core losses dominate, leading to a decrease in efficiency.” look not convincing. Indeed, ad low duty-cycle, the PV panel works at high-voltage/low-current behind the MPP, so that conduction losses are lower compared to high duty-cycle operation, corresponding to low voltage/high-current PV operation beyond MPP.

Please verify and clarify

5) page 4, left column, section III: the plots of Fig.9 is not consistent with the following introducing statements of section III “The performance of the proposed Fuzzy Logic-based Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT) system is rigorously evaluated through comprehensive simulations under dynamically changing solar irradiation conditions. The system is specifically designed to maintain stable operation and accurately track the maximum power point (MPP) even in the presence of significant variations in irradiation levels. This section presents detailed transient simulation results, highlighting the system’s robustness and adaptability to fluctuating environmental conditions.”. The plots pf PV efficiency and converter efficiency of Fig.9 do not allow reading the local values of the efficiency, which looks flattened to 100%. The plot of the output current shows a transition time between the different levels corresponding to different irradiance. During this time, what the P V panel power is? No information is given on the PV panel model and power converter loss model used, nor the simulation results show the most important information: the trajectory of the operating point of the panel, proving how it tracks the MPP during irradiation changes (by the waym, these look step-wise in Fig.9, never happening in reality).

Please provide more useful results and discussion

6) page 4, left column, section III: these statements “The simulation result given by Figure 9 underscores the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed Fuzzy Logic based MPPT system. The system not only tracks the MPP with high accuracy but also adapts seamlessly to sudden changes in solar irradiation. This adaptability is critical for real-world applications, where environmental conditions can vary rapidly and unpredictably. The ability to maintain stable operation under such conditions highlights the practical viability of the proposed system for use in photovoltaic battery charging applications.” are not proved at all. The most important question to answer is: which are the improvements of the proposed fuzzy-logic approach compared to the traditional MPPT methods, such as Perturb and Observe (P&O), which are claimed to “suffer from inefficiencies due to their sensitivity to rapid fluctuations in solar irradiation.” in the section I? The literature of power elec tronics if full of studies proving that a P&O control can be very high efficient if the control parameters are correctly set. To claim a new method is better that P&O it is very opportune to prove that this is true by means of fair and very well documented technical comparisons.

Please provide more useful results and discussion
